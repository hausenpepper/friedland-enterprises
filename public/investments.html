Investment Thesis

LVLU is executing a legitimate operational turnaround — 44.3% gross margins, three consecutive positive EBITDA quarters, and 143% wholesale revenue growth — while trading at 0.12x revenue versus peer REVOLVE Group at 1.3x. The valuation disconnect is driven by governance deficiencies, minimal institutional engagement, and zero analyst coverage, not fundamental business problems.

Friedland Enterprises holds approximately 5% of outstanding shares (disclosed via Schedule 13D, January 2026) and has engaged constructively with management and the board. Our base case models $293M revenue and $3.5M positive EBITDA for FY2026, driven by wholesale channel expansion to ~$29M across 12+ retail partners including Nordstrom (all ~93 stores), Amazon, and Victoria's Secret. At 0.3x P/S — a significant discount to REVOLVE — the stock reaches $30 per share.
